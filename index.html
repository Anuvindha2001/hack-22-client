About  .hack( );

.hack( ); 2022 The 2-day National hackathon is here to mould a generation of developers who are competent and well informed of rapid technological advancements. We bring bright minds from across the country to compete and build solutions for problems they met within day-to-day lives. We help bring out the best in them by conducting events and competitions of exemplary standards on levels ranging from our SB level to international levels. .hack(); 2022 will be a platform to inculcate a culture of product innovation and a mindset of problem-solving. Let's get ready to think out of the box and come up with innovative unique solutions...

Schedule

23rd Nov 6:00 pm

Registration Starts

Registration for the fifth edition of .hack hackathon begins! Click on the "Apply with Devfolio" button on our website and fill out your details. You can always register individually and form a team later. RSVP will be done closer to the hackathon.

20th Dec - 5th Jan

Workshop Series

In the evening informative speaker sessions will be conducted by industry experts. The platform and link for the session will be shared on our website and Discord server before the event date.

26th Dec 6:00 pm

Release of Problem Statements

Problem statements focussing on the recent and trending topics in the field of research and development will be released.

26th Dec - 4th Jan 11:59 pm

Idea Submission

Registered teams will receive a form link after the release of problem statements. They need to select any one of the problem statements and submit an idea on how they are planning to go about their hack. Once an idea is submitted, they will receive the RSVP for the hackathon.

4th Jan 11:59 pm

Registration Ends

This is the end of the registration phase. Teams should ensure all their members have registered on Devfolio before this date.

7th Jan 7:00 pm

Opening Ceremony

The inauguration ceremony of .hack will start. Participants will be briefed on rules and regulations, schedules, and other information regarding the hackathon.

7th Jan 9:00 pm

Virtual Onboarding

Teams will be checked in before the main event commences. Participants are requested to make sure that their team members are present and all the prerequisites for the event are fulfilled.

7th Jan 10:00 pm

Hacking Starts

Coding time starts! The participating teams can utilize their private team rooms on the discord server to discuss and develop their hack based on their chosen problem statement.

9th Jan 10:00 am

Final Submission - Coding Time Ends

This is the end of the 36 hour coding period. Teams will have to submit their final work. After this point, no addition to the project will be allowed.

Frequently Asked  Questions

play_arrowWhat is the goal of the hackathon?

We aim to promote development oriented policies that support productive activities, decent job creation , entrepreneurship, creativity,innovation etc. which align with UN sustainable goals. Thereby substantially increasing the number of youth and adults with relevant skills for employment , jobs and entrepreneurship. We also wish to introduce opportunities that students usually lack, like real life working skills, rapid problem solving skills, thinking in terms of product, engaging in a team event where we support and grow hence providing an exposure to the industry where they get to socialise with experts, take their advice, learn from them and build.


play_arrowWho all can participate? Are there any prerequisite or required skills?

The hackathon is open to all students. Though prior knowledge in coding and hardware would be beneficial , teams would also require members who have great ideas and presentation skills. In case you are a newbie and want to experience a good hackathon, you are at the right place. hack'21 is the apt place for you to start!


play_arrowDo I need a team to participate?

You can participate in .hack(); 2021 individually or as a team. A team can have maximum of 4 members.


play_arrowHow much does it cost?

The registration for the Hackathon is completely free of cost. So all those who are interested are welcome to be a part of .hack(); '21


play_arrowHow is the event hosted?

Due to the pandemic and to ensure the safety of our participants , we have decided to host the hackathon online. All students from anywhere across the globe can participate.


play_arrowWho is organising the hackathon?

The hackathon is organized by IEEE MACE SB.


play_arrowAre there any prizes?

Of course yes! A lot of amazing prizes and cool swags awaits you at hack'21.


play_arrowWhat will be the theme for .hack();?

Not to worry, the problem statements will be based on a variety of theme options.


play_arrowWhat if I've never been to a hackathon before?

Then we're so glad .hack will be your first ever! It’s helpful to have some programming or technical experience, but it’s not a requirement. We’ll have talks, mentors and workshops to help you with your project.


play_arrowWhere can I find updates?

Join us on Discord and follow us on Instagram for all the updates. Also we will be reaching out to you via mail. To receive the updates through mail, kindly register for the hackathon.
